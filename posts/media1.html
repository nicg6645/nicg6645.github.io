Some Media* That Really Clicked With Me in 2020

January 15, 2021, 6:56 PM

*To be precise: a book, two youtube videos and some articles. I’m retroactively excluding music for the sake of brevity. This list is in no particular order.

Eyal Weizman, ‘Lethal Theory’

Venkatesh Rao, ‘Programmable Reality’

Ben Lerner, ‘Ross Perot and China’ - This is excerpted from The Topeka School, which is also great.

Aaron Lewis, ‘The Garden of Forking Memes’

Tom Scocca, ‘On Smarm’

Dylan Matthews, ‘What It’s Like to Give a Kidney to a Stranger’

Dylan Matthews, ‘The Lottery’ and ‘Occupy Recruiting’ - Although I only agree strongly with the former, I decided to include both because they reduce the concept of college into two distinct questions: how to get to it, and how to leave it.

James Gleick, The Information, Chapter Two: ‘The Persistence of the Word (There is no Dictionary in the Mind )’

Venkatesh Rao, ‘On the Design of Escaped Realities’

Kartik Chandra, ‘GPT-3 and Claude Shannon Visit the Tartu-Moscow School’

Joseph Anderson, The Witness - A Great Game That You Shouldn’t Play
